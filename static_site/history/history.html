History of Port Hope Fair

The Port Hope & District Agricultural Society was founded in 1831. Farmers in Hope Township formed the society, and its first steps were taken in November 1830. A subscription list was circulated among those who wanted to become members, and a meeting was held in December 1830. A constitution was drawn up, and the society became a reality in January 1831.

Early fairs were similar to farmers’ markets, where produce could be sold instead of just exhibited. Shorter fairs were sometimes held in the spring, and some years a local fair would be replaced with a larger regional event.

The Port Hope Agricultural Society was renamed the East Durham Agricultural Society in 1839, and in 1846 a provincial society was formed. After the union of Upper and Lower Canada in the 1840s, agricultural societies became more active, and their exhibitions stimulated wider use of agricultural machinery.

The County of Durham was divided into two ridings – East and West – in 1852. Each riding formed its own society under the names of the East and West Durham Agricultural Societies. The East Durham Agricultural Society continued to hold its annual exhibition in Port Hope's Market Square, using the town for exhibiting ladies’ work and fine arts.

In 1875, the Fair obtained a large parcel of land, known as Exhibition Park. Spring fairs of the Hope Township Agricultural Society and union exhibitions of the East Durham and Hope Township Agricultural Societies were held at Exhibition Park until 1889.

The Fair Board obtained grant money from the Province which was used to help with some improvements of the park. In 2002, the hydro extension onto the Trinity College leased lands was paid for by the Agricultural Society.

A Kid’s Guide to the Port Hope Fair was published in 2006. The first Agricultural Education Day for Port Hope’s grade three students was held in 2007 and continues to be held the Friday morning before the opening of each year’s Fair.
